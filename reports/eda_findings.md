# Mutual Fund Analytics - EDA Findings

## 1. SIP Inflows Show Strong Growth
Monthly SIP inflows increased significantly from 2022 to 2025, indicating growing retail participation in mutual funds.

## 2. Record SIP Inflow Achieved in 2025
The highest SIP inflow was observed during 2025, demonstrating strong investor confidence in systematic investing.

## 3. SBI Mutual Fund Maintains AUM Leadership
Among all fund houses, SBI Mutual Fund consistently recorded one of the largest Assets Under Management (AUM).

## 4. Equity-Oriented Categories Attract Higher Inflows
Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap, and Flexi Cap categories received substantial investor inflows compared to other categories.

## 5. Investors Are Concentrated in Working-Age Groups
The majority of investors belong to the 26–35 and 36–45 age groups, reflecting strong participation from salaried professionals.

## 6. Investment Participation Is Distributed Across Genders
Both male and female investors contribute significantly to mutual fund investments, indicating broad market participation.

## 7. Investment Amounts Vary Across Age Groups
Box plot analysis shows differences in transaction sizes across age segments, with several high-value outliers present.

## 8. Mutual Fund Adoption Is Expanding Beyond Metro Cities
The T30 vs B30 analysis indicates meaningful participation from investors outside the top 30 cities.

## 9. Industry Folio Count Has Grown Consistently
Total folios increased substantially between 2022 and 2025, highlighting rising awareness and adoption of mutual funds.

## 10. Several Funds Exhibit Strong Positive Correlation
The correlation matrix shows that many funds move together, suggesting exposure to common market factors.
